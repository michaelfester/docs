## Adding a project

To create a new app, click on the "New Project" button to get started.

{% figure src="/docs/reference/account/projects/new-project-button.png" caption="New Project button" /%}

When you click on the "New Project" button, you will need to enter your project's name and choose which type of project — [Apps](/docs/apps) or [Pages](/docs/pages).

{% figure src="/docs/reference/account/projects/adding-an-app.gif" caption="Adding an app" /%}

### Data source

On the next step, you need to choose your [Data Source](/docs/reference/data-sources). You can choose an external Data Source, or you can use \*\*Glide's built-in Tables.

When you choose an external Data Source, you will be required to select a sheet from your Google Drive. Glide will then populate your app with data from your sheet.

When you choose Glide Tables, you can populate your data manually once the app is created. Check the guide below on how to use Glide Tables:

{% cta title="Glide Tables" description="Learn how to use Glide's built-in data source" url="/docs/glide-tables" /%}

## Deleting a project

To delete an app, go to your [dashboard](/docs/glide-ui) and hover over the app that you want to delete. Click on the three-dot menu on the right corner and click on "Delete."

{% figure src="/docs/reference/account/projects/deleting-an-app.gif" caption="Deleting an app" /%}

{% callout type="warning" title="Deleting an app" %}
Deleting an app is a permanent action and cannot be undone. For back-up purposes, you can duplicate your app or export your app data.
{% /callout %}

### How to duplicate an app

To duplicate an app, locate the app of your choice on your dashboard, click on the options button, and choose duplicate.

To create a separate sheet for that duplicated app, choose Copy the Sheet from the options and then click on Duplicate App to confirm. You also have the option to use the same sheet for that app.

Once an app is duplicated, it will be named "Copy of APPNAME" as a default. You can rename your app later in the App Info setting.

{% figure src="/docs/reference/account/projects/renaming-an-app.gif" caption="Renaming an app" /%}

## Transferring projects to a team

To transfer a project to another team, click on the three-dot menu on the app tile and select the team you wish to move the app to.

{% figure src="/docs/reference/account/projects/transferring-an-app.gif" caption="Transferring an app" /%}

If you want to keep a copy of a project in your current team, make sure to duplicate it first and then transfer it to the team afterward.

When you move a project to another team, all members will be able to access it and any sheet that it is connected to. The billing for that app will also be migrated to the new team.

Only admins can move projects out of a team. Check the members area of your team to see who is an admin.
